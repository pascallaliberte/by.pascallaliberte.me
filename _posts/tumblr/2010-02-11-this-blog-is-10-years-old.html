Ten years ago, I dreamed up a design for a personal site.  It would have had my resume, my contact information, a news portion where I would add my thoughts and a sort of experimental section where I’d try out php and mysql code I’d work on.

Of course, I wasn’t the first one to think of the idea of having that kind of site, after all, there have been blogs around for 11 years or so now, but in a way I feel like I was onto something back then.

And yet, I just got my blog started.  So I’m late in the game for a guy who has been making websites for 13 years.

Prior to this blog, the following has been my footprint on the web:

  • University of Ottawa — Where I currently work.  I do web stuff there: support web systems for other webmasters, do front-end coding on top-level pages and developed the templates used by the 90+ website owners.
  • pascallaliberte.info — My personal site.

In 20 years, many variables may change in computing.  Cables will have largely disappeared, the notion that you have to move to a certain location to get access to information will look ridiculous, but one variable won’t change: the size of the device matters.

You’ll want a personal device that fits in your pocket, which you can hold to your ear for a private conversation with a remote person. You’ll want something larger that you can consume most of your favourite information on, and even do most of your work on.  And maybe you’ll have a place where you sit down to work with a device.

But you’ll still want a device that’s big enough so you can comfortably sit in front of with friends and family.

All those device sizes will still matter.  You’ll accomplish largely the same kind of task on each device, but you’ll accomplish those tasks differently on each.

The new ATV will do away with its current OS X-lite variation as a operating system, and will instead adopt the iPhone OS for the underlying experience. There’s no word at this point on whether apps and the App Store will be coming along for the ride, but it makes sense given the shared platform. 

Seems to me like Apple sees it the same way.

Last night, I gave a presentation at TeamCamp on Google Analytics.

Slides

While my presentation was geared to be more of a long Q&A session, I used the following slides to cover the basics of Google Analytics in the first half.

Thanks for all who participated, asked questions, and volunteered to demo their sites for the presentation.

Google Analytics - Teamcamp Feb 18 2010

Topics covered during the presentation

  • Bounce Rate
  • Conversions
  • Setting goals
  • Campaigns — see the Campaign URL builder tool
  • Excluding your own visits from the statistics
  • Profiles: suggested using a Test Profile for testing filters, a Raw data profile (no filters) and separating traffic to your dev site from your production site
  • Bilingual sites, bilingual domains: best practices for setting up profiles, suggested using a single tracking code
  • Filters
  • Digging through your data: using Advanced Segments, getting contextual answers about a page (e.g. geographical source of visitors visiting this particular page)
  • Annotations
  • Click tracking (Event tracking): how it helps get a better bounce rate

A few of the shortcomings of Google Analytics:

  • Time on site doesn’t take into account bounced visits
  • The Site overlay isn’t as accurate as tools like CrazyEgg and Clicktale

Also discussed other tools:

  • Mint — 30$, installable on your own server, upgradable via plugins (Peppers), and has neat ways to expose your visitor info (e.g. Newest Unique Referrers are real clever)
  • Crazy Egg — Generates heat maps of the clicks on your page
  • ClickTale — Record playable videos of mouse movements and keystrokes of your visitors.  Also generates great heat maps and allows you to track the effectiveness and drop-out points for filling online forms.
  • Google Website Optimizer — Allows you to compare the effectiveness of two (or more) different designs or content versions (A/B testing and multivariate testing).

Thanks for all who came.  I had a great time, and it was nice meeting you all.

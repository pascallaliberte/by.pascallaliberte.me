Following up on my post from early today, something’s not sitting right with the keynote for me. Steve Jobs was a little too melancholic yesterday, giving his thanks to everyone on the team, making a big deal with the finally-we-got-it-working video phone.

It’s been a tough year for him, to be sure, avoiding death and all. But look at the keynote with the perspective that 3 months from now, we learn that he will no longer be running the company. See how it feels as though he’s giving this keynote as though it may be his last one?

It would be a perfect ending.  He’s going to be credited a lot of things, and not the least of which, two form factors that will have revolutionized mobile communication devices and computing through the iPhone and the iPad and now, realizing a childhood dream of his, to bring video calling to reality.  He could stop here and he could make this keynote his last one and he would be leaving on a high note.  And looking in hindsight a few months or years from now you could say I told you so.

We already know that Steve lives each day as if it were his last one, but that didn’t stop him from delivering all his keynotes on the assumption he’d continue running the ship for longer.  I’m sensing, this time, that he’s sending a subtle look-at-my-life’s-contribution message and a subtler I’m-passing-the-torch moment at the end.

But who am I kidding.  Steve’s not going anywhere.

Right?
